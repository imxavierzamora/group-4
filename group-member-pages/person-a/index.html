Xavier Zamora and the Order of the Fourth Estate

Xavier Zamora is no ordinary journalist — he is a seasoned chronicler of the Muggle world's most hidden truths, wielding a camera and a notebook with the precision of a wand. Having spent nearly two decades documenting the stories of the forgotten and the overlooked, he has now enrolled at the hallowed halls of UC Berkeley's Graduate School of Journalism, a place whispered about in newsrooms the way Hogwarts is whispered about in wizarding families. His specialties — data journalism, investigative reporting, and documentary photography — are said to be as rare a combination as Parseltongue paired with Legilimency.

When he is not filing dispatches for EdSource or wandering the record shops of San Francisco — where vinyl collectors gather like a secret order protecting ancient artifacts of sound — Xavier can be found behind a lens at a ballpark, capturing the quiet drama of Triple-A baseball with the patience of a wizard awaiting the perfect moment to cast. He carries with him the authority of someone who has survived the collapse of old institutions and emerged not bitter, but sharper — like a spell cast under pressure that turns out to be precisely the right one.
